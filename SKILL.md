# Universal Motivation Architecture

This skill pack distills Octalysis into a general motivation-design and behavior-diagnosis architecture.

The core is not "make it game-like." The core is:

> Diagnose the motivation structure behind a behavior, then design a more natural, durable, feedback-rich, and ethically bounded experience.

## Operating Principles

1. Start with target behavior and success metric, not mechanics.
2. Diagnose CD1-CD8 as motivation signals, not labels.
3. Separate intrinsic/extrinsic and white-hat/black-hat tradeoffs.
4. Map motivation by journey stage.
5. Treat cases as transferable patterns with non-transferable context.
6. Review risky mechanisms before recommending scarcity, randomness, streaks, social pressure, or loss avoidance.

## Skill Routing

- Use `skills/strategy-dashboard` when goals, actors, target behaviors, feedback, and rewards are not yet defined.
- Use `skills/motivation-diagnosis` when the problem is why people start, stop, avoid, return, or over-engage.
- Use `skills/core-drive-intervention` when a diagnosis needs concrete design moves.
- Use `skills/journey-stage-mapping` when motivation changes across first contact, onboarding, repeated use, and maturity.
- Use `skills/feedback-loop-design` when a behavior must repeat and self-reinforce.
- Use `skills/mission-narrative-design` when the behavior needs meaning, identity, shared mission, or credible purpose.
- Use `skills/case-pattern-transfer` when adapting a case without copying surface mechanics.
- Use `skills/motivation-ethics-review` for scarcity, FOMO, streaks, random rewards, social pressure, sunk cost, loss, lock-in, or external incentives.

## Default Output Shape

For diagnosis/design tasks, output:

1. Target behavior and success metric.
2. Current motivation diagnosis.
3. Missing, overused, or risky drives.
4. Design strategy.
5. Concrete interventions or loop.
6. Ethical guardrails.
7. Measurement plan.

## Non-Negotiables

- Do not reduce the work to points, badges, leaderboards, rewards, or streaks.
- Do not use black-hat mechanisms without risk review.
- Do not treat business metrics as the only valid success metrics.
- Do not copy cases by surface form.
- Do not ignore ability, access, trust, safety, health, money, or policy constraints.
