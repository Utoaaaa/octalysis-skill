# Glossary And Language Map

## Core Glossary

| Term | Generic Pack Term | Operational Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Gamification | Motivation architecture | Designing the motivational structure behind a behavior, not making something look like a game. |
| Human-focused design | Human-centered motivation design | Design for feelings, uncertainty, insecurity, reasons to act, and reasons not to act. |
| Function-focused design | Function-first system design | Assumes people already want to act and only need efficiency/usability. |
| Core drive | Motivation driver | A psychologically distinct reason a person takes action. |
| Player | Motivated actor / participant | The person or group whose behavior is being diagnosed or designed for. |
| Player type | Motivation segment | A group with a different dominant motivation profile for the same target behavior. |
| Business metric | Success metric / target outcome | The prioritized measurable result the design must improve. |
| Desired action | Target behavior | The concrete behavior that moves the success metric. |
| Win state | Completion state / effective behavior closure | A state where the actor has completed the behavior and receives meaningful feedback. |
| Game loop | Self-reinforcing behavior loop | A loop where feedback, asset, ability, status, or relationship strengthens re-entry into the target behavior. |
| Reward | Feedback / reinforcement / rationalization asset | Something that confirms meaning, progress, ownership, social value, possibility, or urgency. |
| Trigger | Re-entry cue / activation point | A cue that invites the next target behavior at the right time and motivation state. |
| Loyalty | Long-term relationship / identity attachment | Stable connection to behavior, group, identity, or system. |
| Referral | Voluntary advocacy | A participant chooses to bring others because the experience supports identity, usefulness, or social value. |
| Conversion | Target behavior completion | A specific action completed in a journey stage. |
| Retention | Repeated behavior | Continued return to the behavior over time. |
| Brand mission | Meaning narrative | A credible link between action and a larger purpose. |
| Points/badges/leaderboards | Progress/status artifacts | Surface artifacts that only work when attached to real motivation. |

## Core Drives

| Code | Canonical Name | Generic Diagnosis |
|---|---|---|
| CD1 | Epic Meaning And Calling / 重大使命與呼召 | Does the actor feel the behavior connects to meaning, identity, or a larger mission? |
| CD2 | Development And Accomplishment / 發展與成就 | Does the actor see progress, challenge, mastery, and completion? |
| CD3 | Empowerment Of Creativity And Feedback / 賦予創造力與回饋 | Does the actor have choice, experimentation, expression, and responsive feedback? |
| CD4 | Ownership And Possession / 所有權與佔有欲 | Does the behavior create assets, personalization, accumulated investment, or stewardship? |
| CD5 | Social Influence And Relatedness / 社交影響力與同理心 | Does the actor feel belonging, recognition, trust, reciprocity, or meaningful comparison? |
| CD6 | Scarcity And Impatience / 稀缺性與迫切 | Is there a meaningful window, constraint, or unavailable-but-desired state? |
| CD7 | Unpredictability And Curiosity / 不確定性與好奇心 | Is there discovery, surprise, mystery, or unresolved information? |
| CD8 | Loss And Avoidance / 損失與避免 | Is action driven by avoiding loss, regret, FOMO, sunk cost, or negative consequences? |

## Axis Map

| Axis | Drives | Useful For | Watch Out |
|---|---|---|---|
| White hat | CD1, CD2, CD3 | Long-term energy, meaning, mastery, autonomy | Often lacks urgency; can stay aspirational. |
| Black hat | CD6, CD7, CD8 | Short-term urgency, return, focus, action | Can create fatigue, anxiety, addiction, or loss of agency. |
| Left-brain / extrinsic | CD2, CD4, CD6 | Goals, assets, achievement, scarcity | Can crowd out intrinsic motivation if overused. |
| Right-brain / intrinsic | CD3, CD5, CD7 | Creativity, social meaning, curiosity, experience | Can be harder to measure and harder to force. |
| Mixed / context-dependent | CD4, CD5 | Ownership and social influence can empower or pressure | Check whether the actor feels stronger or trapped. |

## Journey Map

| Original Stage | Generic Stage | Motivation Question |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery | First contact | Why would someone care enough to try? |
| Onboarding | Start action | How do they quickly feel capable, oriented, and safe? |
| Scaffolding | Daily/repeated behavior | Why would they keep re-entering the loop? |
| Endgame | Long-term maturity or fatigue | Why stay after novelty and easy progress fade? |

## Boundary Notes

- Do not call every participant a "player" in user-facing outputs unless the context is explicitly game/product oriented.
- Do not translate every behavior problem into "add a reward"; reward-only design is a failure mode.
- Do not treat a high score on one drive as always good. Overuse and wrong-context use can harm the behavior.
- Do not use the framework to override practical constraints: ability, money, time, access, health, trust, policy, and safety may be the real blockers.
