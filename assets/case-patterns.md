# Case Pattern Library

This library stores reusable patterns and transfer warnings. Use cases as mechanism examples, not templates.

## Patagonia — Credible Sacrifice Mission

- **Context**: Apparel brand during Black Friday.
- **Behavior problem**: Avoid commodity discount race and prove environmental commitment.
- **Missing/dominant drives**: CD1 weak in retail; buying dominated by CD4/CD6.
- **Intervention drives**: CD1, CD7, CD5.
- **Mechanics**: Anti-purchase message, environmental impact facts, long-term donations, repair and secondhand encouragement.
- **Why it worked**: The anti-sales stance made the mission costly and therefore credible.
- **Transferable pattern**: Make mission believable through visible sacrifice.
- **Non-transferable context**: Requires real operational proof and brand permission to challenge consumption.
- **Misuse warning**: Fake sacrifice becomes hypocrisy.

## Waze — Shared-World Repair

- **Context**: Navigation utility with user-generated traffic data.
- **Behavior problem**: Passive GPS use and early data imperfection.
- **Intervention drives**: CD1, CD3, CD4, CD5, CD7.
- **Mechanics**: Traffic monster framing, community reporting, thanks, avatars, learned preferences.
- **Why it worked**: Users became defenders and contributors, not just consumers.
- **Transferable pattern**: Frame contribution as repairing a shared world.
- **Non-transferable context**: Needs network effects and live feedback.
- **Misuse warning**: Do not incentivize unsafe or unnecessary real-world behavior.

## Chipotle — Interaction Performs Brand Claim

- **Context**: Food brand marketing and trust repair.

- **Intervention drives**: CD1, CD3, CD7, CD5.
- **Mechanics**: Story/game around ethical sourcing; spelling-bee format contrasting simple ingredients and chemical names.
- **Why it worked**: Participation reinforced the actual brand promise.

## Autodesk — Product Trial As Challenge

- **Behavior problem**: Users need to try, learn, and see power before buying.
- **Intervention drives**: CD3, CD2.
- **Mechanics**: Missions requiring the trial software to solve problems.

## Caixa / Brazil Bank — Measurable Shared Quest

- **Context**: Large bank with tens of thousands of employees.
- **Behavior problem**: Align roles around profit improvement and recurring behavior.
- **Intervention drives**: CD1-CD8.
- **Mechanics**: Named quest, mission cards, collectible fragments, trading, timed unlocks, instant rewards.
- **Why it worked**: Company goal became visible, collectible, social, and role-linked.
- **Transferable pattern**: Name a measurable shared quest and bind each role's target behavior to it.
- **Non-transferable context**: Requires executive mandate and data plumbing.
- **Misuse warning**: Large rewards need economic control.

## HTC / VIVE — Funnel Hygiene Plus Fandom Loop

- **Context**: VR brand with weak ecommerce and passionate niche.
- **Behavior problem**: Rebuild fan energy and conversion across funnel.
- **Intervention drives**: CD3, CD5, CD7, plus clear CD2 win states.
- **Mechanics**: Stronger CTAs, choice framing, social proof, playful replies, influencer status, mystery teasers, ARG.
- **Why it worked**: Fans felt seen and had status, mystery, and co-creation.
- **Transferable pattern**: Fix basic target actions before adding advanced fandom loops.
- **Non-transferable context**: Needs responsive brand voice and engaged niche.
- **Misuse warning**: ARGs fail if the basic funnel is broken.

## Woot — Appointment Drop Ritual

- **Context**: Daily-deal ecommerce.
- **Behavior problem**: Make surplus/limited products habit-forming.
- **Intervention drives**: CD6, CD7.
- **Mechanics**: One product at midnight, limited stock, humorous copy, mystery deals.
- **Why it worked**: Scarcity and reveal trained a return ritual.
- **Transferable pattern**: Appointment-based drops can build habit.
- **Non-transferable context**: Needs real scarcity and entertaining reveal.
- **Misuse warning**: Disappointing products burn trust.

## Quibids — Anti-Pattern: Zero-Sum Sunk Cost

- **Context**: Penny auction.
- **Behavior problem**: Monetize bidding itself.
- **Overused drives**: CD6, CD7, CD8.
- **Mechanics**: Paid bids, low apparent prices, sunk-cost escalation.
- **Why it worked short-term**: Users kept bidding to avoid losing prior spend.
- **Transferable pattern**: As caution only: every loop needs acceptable win states for most participants.
- **Misuse warning**: If one winner creates many resentful losers, retention and reputation collapse.
