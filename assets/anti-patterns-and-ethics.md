# Anti-Patterns And Ethics

## Core Ethical Standard

Use motivation architecture to help actors do things they would endorse on reflection.

Do not create urgency, uncertainty, shame, sunk cost, or social pressure to push people toward actions they do not need, cannot afford, cannot safely perform, or would resent once pressure is removed.

## Mandatory Review For High-Risk Mechanisms

If a design uses any of the following, produce an ethics review:

- scarcity, exclusivity, waitlists, countdowns,
- streaks, loss of progress, FOMO, penalties,
- variable rewards, mystery boxes, random prizes,
- social comparison, peer pressure, public rankings,
- sunk-cost investment, collections, lock-in,
- continuous reminders, push notifications, re-entry loops,
- monetary rewards, discounts, cash-equivalent points.

## Required Outputs For Risky Designs

For each risky mechanism, state:

1. Short-term benefit.
2. Long-term fatigue risk.
3. Loss-of-agency risk.
4. Intrinsic-motivation crowd-out risk.
5. Safety, financial, social, or mental-health risk.
6. White-hat alternative or balancing design.
7. Exit, pause, recovery, or opt-out path.

## Anti-Pattern Catalog

### PBL Cargo-Culting

- **Signal**: "Add points, badges, and a leaderboard" is the entire proposal.
- **Mechanism**: Surface artifacts are treated as motivation.
- **Harm**: Users feel patronized; behavior does not improve.
- **Alternative**: Diagnose target behavior and core drives first.

### Mercenary Rewards

- **Signal**: Users only care about reward rate.
- **Mechanism**: CD4 stuff/cash replaces relationship.
- **Harm**: Users leave for better subsidies.
- **Alternative**: Add status, access, power, identity, community, and trust.

### Overjustification

- **Signal**: A previously enjoyable behavior now requires compensation.
- **Mechanism**: External reward reframes intrinsic activity as work.
- **Harm**: Motivation drops when reward shrinks or stops.
- **Alternative**: Preserve autonomy, creativity, curiosity, recognition, and social meaning.

### Black-Hat Fatigue

- **Signal**: Users keep acting but feel trapped, anxious, or relieved when they quit.
- **Mechanism**: CD6/CD7/CD8 dominate without white-hat recovery.
- **Harm**: Burnout, churn, resentment.
- **Alternative**: Use pressure only in time-bounded, user-endorsed contexts.

### Variable Reward Compulsion

- **Signal**: Users repeatedly check, open, bid, or pull despite low value.
- **Mechanism**: Uncertainty and intermittent rewards override agency.
- **Harm**: Gambling-like loops, compulsive behavior.
- **Alternative**: Bound randomness, show limits, keep stakes low, avoid vulnerable domains.

### Social Norm Backfire

- **Signal**: Message says many similar people do the bad thing.
- **Mechanism**: Descriptive norm normalizes the bad behavior.
- **Harm**: Bad behavior increases.
- **Alternative**: Truthfully show similar people doing the desired behavior.

### Sunk-Cost Prison

- **Signal**: Users continue because leaving would waste prior investment.
- **Mechanism**: CD4 ownership plus CD8 loss avoidance.
- **Harm**: Trapped use, resentment, inability to exit.
- **Alternative**: Portability, graceful exit, pause, refund, or preserved progress.

### Scarcity Deception

- **Signal**: False "limited time" or "exclusive" claims.
- **Mechanism**: Artificial CD6 urgency.
- **Harm**: Trust erosion and legal/regulatory risk.
- **Alternative**: Use real constraints and disclose them.

### Unsafe Spillover

- **Signal**: Motivation loop changes real-world movement, spending, health, driving, or social exposure.
- **Mechanism**: Designed incentives create unintended risky behavior.
- **Harm**: Physical, financial, environmental, or social harm.
- **Alternative**: Pre-launch unintended-consequence review and hard guardrails.
