# Mission Narrative Design

## R — 原文 (Reading)

> "關鍵是可信度。"
>
> — Section II, CD1 設計原則

## I — 方法論骨架 (Interpretation)

Mission narrative design is CD1 intervention: connecting a behavior to meaning, identity, contribution, or a shared future.

The method distinguishes real vs fictional and existing vs inspired purpose. The resulting modes are meaning, vision, fable, and myth.

Mission only works when credible. Credibility comes from good story, sincere belief, visible sacrifice, community reinforcement, and continuous feedback.

The design must link the mission to a concrete target behavior. Otherwise it is branding copy, not motivation architecture.

## A1 — 書中的應用 (Past Application)

### 案例 1: Patagonia

- **問題**: Retail buying normally becomes price, style, and consumption.
- **方法論的使用**: Patagonia tied purchase and non-purchase to environmental mission, with costly proof.
- **結論**: Sacrifice made the mission credible.
- **結果**: Source cites sales increase despite the anti-purchase message.

### 案例 2: Ocean Hero

- **問題**: Search utility was inferior to Google for many uses.
- **方法論的使用**: Searching became ocean plastic recovery, and research mode made the tradeoff transparent.
- **結論**: Users can accept friction when mission and cost are visible.
- **結果**: Source cites major bottle recovery growth.

### 案例 3: Waze

- **問題**: Navigation is a functional tool.
- **方法論的使用**: Users were framed as helping a community fight traffic.
- **結論**: A tool can become a shared mission when use contributes to others.

## A2 — 觸發場景 (Future Trigger)

### 用户會在什麼情境下需要這個 skill?

1. A behavior feels like a chore or transaction.
2. A team, course, community, or workflow lacks a shared direction.
3. A brand or product wants purpose without sounding fake.
4. A personal habit needs identity and long-term meaning.
5. Users need to see why an action matters beyond immediate reward.

### 語言信號

- "這件事只是雜事，怎麼讓它有意義?"
- "我們要設計使命/願景/社群方向。"
- "怎麼讓使用者覺得自己在參與更大的事?"
- "不要像口號，要可信。"
- "這個習慣要連到身份。"

## E — 可執行步驟 (Execution)

1. **Define the mission target behavior**
   - Name the action the mission should support.
   - Completion standard: the mission is tied to an observable behavior.

2. **Choose mission type**
   - Classify as real/existing meaning, real/inspired vision, fictional/existing fable, or fictional/inspired myth.
   - Completion standard: the selected type matches audience belief and context.

3. **Build credibility**
   - Identify proof, sacrifice, track record, transparency, and constraints.
   - Completion standard: at least one costly or verifiable credibility signal exists.

4. **Design reinforcement**
   - Add feedback that shows cumulative impact, identity, group progress, or contribution after repeated actions.
   - Completion standard: the mission appears beyond the first introduction.

5. **Check misuse**
   - Test for hypocrisy, moral pressure, exclusion, and hidden tradeoffs.
   - Completion standard: the actor can reject the mission without being shamed.

## B — 邊界 (Boundary)

### 不要在以下情況使用此 skill

- The organization cannot provide proof, sacrifice, or honest tradeoff.
- The audience needs functional reliability first; mission cannot compensate for core failure.
- The mission would pressure vulnerable users into unwanted action.

### 作者在書中警告的失敗模式

- A brand with low credibility claiming noble purpose can insult users.
- Fictional framing fails when the setting is serious or audience rejects play.
- CD1 is powerful but often lacks urgency, so it may need other drives for action.

### 作者的盲點 / 時代局限

- Source examples often involve brands and public causes; personal and internal workflows need smaller, more grounded identity narratives.

### 容易混淆的鄰近方法論

- Copywriting creates message.
- Mission narrative design creates a credible link between action, identity, contribution, and feedback.
